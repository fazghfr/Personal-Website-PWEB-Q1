Cilegon

City of Steel

Cilegon, Indonesia, the "City of Steel," epitomizes the nation's industrial might. Situated on Java Island's northwest coast, it's a hub for steel production with towering factories and mills. Cilegon's strategic location, and skilled workforce drive Indonesia's steel manufacturing. Not only steel, it has many other industries in this ciry. It symbolizes strength, resilience, and progress, juxtaposed with coastal beauty, blending industry and nature.

About this City

Cilegon is situated on the northwestern coast of Java Island, Indonesia. It is strategically positioned in the Banten province, approximately 100 kilometers (62 miles) west of Jakarta, the capital city of Indonesia. Its coastal location provides access to the Java Sea and plays a vital role in its industrial and logistical significance.


Cilegon boasts a diverse demographic profile, with a population that includes various ethnicities and cultures. The city is predominantly inhabited by Javanese and Sundanese people, who make up the majority of the population. Additionally, due to its industrial significance, Cilegon attracts a considerable number of migrants and workers from other regions of Indonesia, contributing to its multicultural makeup. This demographic diversity has enriched the city's social fabric and contributes to its vibrant and dynamic atmosphere.
